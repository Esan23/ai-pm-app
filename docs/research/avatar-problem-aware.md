## Stage of Awareness: **Problem Aware**

Daniel knows, viscerally, that *something is broken* in how his team runs AI work. He feels the symptom — chaos, rework, missed context, projects that quietly drift — every single day. What he does **not** yet believe is that a purpose-built, AI-native project management tool exists or is the answer. In his mind, the problem is generic tooling (Jira wasn't built for this), or worse, a personal failure of discipline. He's still searching for *why it hurts*, not yet shopping for *what fixes it*.

### D → Smart Market Questions

**What keeps them awake at night, eyes open, staring at the ceiling:**
The nagging certainty that *something important is slipping through the cracks right now and he doesn't know what it is.* Three AI experiments are in flight, half the context lives in Slack threads, ChatGPT chats, and his own head, and he cannot get a single honest answer to "what's actually the status of everything?" He lies awake replaying the project that died and feeling it about to happen again.

**What are they secretly afraid of in life:**
That he's a great *builder* but a failing *leader* — that the moment a project requires coordination instead of code, he loses control. Deeper still: that AI is moving so fast he'll wake up one day having shipped a pile of disconnected demos instead of a real product, and everyone will see it.

**What are they angry about, and who are they angry at:**
Angry at his tools — Jira feels like it was designed by people who've never built software, let alone AI. Angry at the "AI roadmap" pressure from leadership that comes with zero structure to deliver it. Quietly angry at *himself* for not having a system, and for confusing being busy with being in control.

**Top 3 frustrations they feel every day:**
1. Re-explaining the same project context across Slack, standups, and three AI chat tools because nothing connects
2. Tasks and user stories living in five places — Jira, Notion, a spreadsheet, his head — none of them current
3. AI-generated work (Copilot PRs, ChatGPT specs, Gemini research) that produces *output* but never lands cleanly back into the plan as trackable progress

**Biggest secret desire in life:**
To be the leader people point to and say *"he made shipping AI look easy."* To have a calm, legible system where he could open one view, see the truth of every project, and spend his energy on the work instead of the wrangling.

**Built-in bias to how they make decisions:**
First-principles and evidence-driven, but heavily anchored on *peer signal* — if respected engineers on HN or his network use it, he trusts it. Deeply skeptical of marketing language and "AI-powered" claims; assumes most tools are hype until proven. Will tolerate friction in setup if the payoff in leverage is real.

**Common words or language unique to them:**
"Source of truth," "context-switching," "this doesn't scale," "shipping velocity," "WIP," "tech debt," "where does this live?," "who owns this?," "ground truth," "good enough to ship."

**Top 3 complaints about existing solutions:**
1. "Jira/Linear weren't built for AI projects — they assume a tidy linear sprint, not messy experimentation"
2. "Notion is flexible but it's a graveyard — nothing stays updated and nothing is trackable"
3. "Everything is disconnected from where the work actually happens (the model, the IDE, the AI chats)"

### F → Purchasing Habits

**Top 3 Decision Triggers:**
1. A respected peer or engineer he follows says "this changed how we ship AI"
2. A painful, concrete failure (a project drifts or dies) makes the status quo intolerable
3. A frictionless way to *see value fast* — a real workflow working in minutes, not a 3-week migration

**Prior Purchases For This Pain:** Jira/Linear seats, Notion, a project-tracking spreadsheet template he paid for, ChatGPT Plus / Copilot subscriptions, possibly an automation tool (Zapier/n8n) to duct-tape things together.

**Price Tolerance For Offer:** High relative to price — $25/user/mo is a rounding error against the cost of one stalled AI project. But he won't pay for *promise*; he pays after he sees it work on his own messy reality.

**Time Horizon Of Solution:** Wants relief *this quarter.* Tolerates a learning curve only if value is visible within the first session.
